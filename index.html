How the Simulation Works

Overview

Two colonies of ants learn to forage using evolutionary optimization. Each ant is controlled by a tiny neural network (no backpropagation); we optimize its weights using either a Genetic Algorithm (GA) or Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO).

Neural "Brain"

  • Inputs (5): food angle (relative), normalized distance, velocity X, velocity Y, bias (1.0)
  • Hidden layer: 4 neurons (tanh)
  • Outputs (2): turn angle (−π/4…π/4), speed (0…max)

Episode and Fitness

  • One generation = 300 frames. All ants act simultaneously.
  • Fitness increases by +10 whenever an ant collects a food item.
  • At generation end, GA/PSO produce the next set of brains; ants respawn.

Genetic Algorithm (GA)

  • Selection: Tournament of random candidates; pick the best.
  • Crossover: Single-point on flattened weight vectors.
  • Mutation: Adds Gaussian noise; elite (best) always survives.

Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO)

  • Each ant = particle with position (weights) and velocity.
  • Updates use inertia + pull toward personal and global best weights.
  • Velocities and weights are clamped to keep the search stable.

Tips

  • Increase inertia to explore; increase cognitive/social to exploit.
  • Mutation rate affects GA diversity; too high can cause churn.
  • Click the canvas to add food and make learning signals denser.
